Work Samples

CX Operations for State Department

Consular Affairs never had CX Operations and we were starting from scratch. My role was the product manager for the first ever CX Operations team for Consular Affairs in the State Department. I supervised a six person team of service designers and content strategists and was the liaison and subject matter expert for four work streams totalling 70 personnel. I introduced agile product development using Jira and trained up teams which resulted in 100% adoption in three months. I worked with leadership to create the first ever three year roadmap aligning strategy and objectives. I oversaw the development of guidance and templates for the entire research process for all work streams, reducing prep time for research projects by 30%.

Digital identity migration strategy for Veteran Affairs

Veteran Affairs needed 16 million Veterans to start using either Login.gov or ID.me when logging into VA.gov or risk getting locked out. We had 12 months. I was the product manager and design lead of a cross-functional agile product team. I led the creation of a centralized dashboard featuring sign-in activity, a communication campaign, and a custom digital service to enable in-person identity verification in partnership with Login.gov, ID.me and National Institute of Standards and Technology. These efforts resulted in 4 million Veterans adopting new credentials within six months, achieving 70% of the target ahead of schedule. The time to create a verified account was reduced from 45 minutes to 10 minutes. I increased sprint velocity by 50%.

Designing with community in mind for UPMC

UPMC wanted to design areas for community members and families but did not understand their needs or what needed to be built. I led a six person research team using qualitative and quantitative research to understand the user experience of patients, families, providers, and local community members. The research methods I designed won a 2022 Evidence-Based Design Touchstone Award, Gold Standard and informed the design of private rest areas and shared community spaces for a $1.5 billion hospital.

Predictive alert system for Philips

Philips, valued at $13 billion at the time, wanted to introduce predictive analytics as a feature but did not know what problem the new feature should address. I served as the product manager for a 24 person team of designers, data scientists, and software engineers. I led the team to create a smart communication platform using predictive analytics to reduce emergency visits for patients with respiratory diseases. I presented the strategy, recommendations, and product to executive leadership which led to multi million dollar portfolio investments for the company.

Increasing patient access to HIV prevention for Montefiore Medical Center

Montefiore Medical Center did not understand why there were low prescription rates of Pre-Exposure Prophylaxis, an HIV prevention method, and low demand signal from patients. This was unexpected as the medical system was located in a geographical area with the highest rates of HIV in the United States. I trained a research team of six to use mixed methods to better understand this anomaly. The research and recommendations I identified led to continued investment of $3 million in the school based health clinics, revisions to NYC Department of Health policy to increase access to HIV prevention methods in primary care settings, and increased provider training. Our research was published in two academic journals and presented at two conferences.
